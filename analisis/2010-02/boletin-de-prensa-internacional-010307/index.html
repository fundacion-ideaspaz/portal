Explota carro bomba en Neiva
El Comercio (Quito) Cuatro heridos dejó una explosión de coche bomba en Colombia

Cuatro personas resultaron gravemente heridas hoy por la explosión de un coche bomba en el centro de la ciudad colombiana de Neiva, 325 km al suroeste de Bogotá, que provocó además daños a vehículos y edificaciones, informó la alcaldesa de esa localidad.La alcaldesa Cielo González manifestó que las personas afectadas se desplazaban en un autobús de transporte público que pasaba por el lugar cuando explotó el coche bomba. ver>>


Líderes paramilitares amenazan con continuar exponiendo sus vínculos con la política
The Financial Times (Londres) Colombian rebels threaten to expose political links
By Andy Webb-Vidal in Bogotá

Salvatore Mancuso, a top commander of Colombia’s United Self-Defence Forces, an outlawed paramilitary army, sleeps in an under-sized bunk in a small cell at Itagüí maximum-security prison near Medellín. And he is not in a good mood. Over the past three years Mr Mancuso and some 31,000 fellow combatants have handed over their weapons as part of a flag­ship plan by Alvaro Uribe, Colombia’s president, to pacify a nation scarred by years of internecine violence. ver>>


Gobierno Colombiano se compromete con la investigación de la Masacre de San José de Apartadó
The New York Times (Nueva York) Colombia Announces Probe Into Massacre

BOGOTA, Colombia (AP) -- Just days ahead of a visit by President Bush, Colombia said it was investigating 69 soldiers accused in a massacre two years ago that induced Washington to suspend $70 million in military aid to the South American nation. While U.S. lawmakers said the move was a sign that the case was making progress at long last, friends and relatives of the victims said the government was only trying to free up the frozen aid for its long-running fight against leftist rebels. ver>>
